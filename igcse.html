Requirements

What students should prepare before starting

Academic Readiness

Schools usually look for students who can manage structured study, multiple subjects, and regular assessment with consistency.

  • Steady performance in previous school years
  • Comfort with English-medium learning or support planning
  • Ability to keep up with subject-by-subject coursework
  • Good learning habits and time management
  • Readiness for a more independent academic routine

Subject Selection Guidance

The most important decision is often not just entry, but choosing subjects that keep future pathways open.

  • Keep mathematics and language strength where possible
  • Include sciences if STEM may be a future option
  • Match optional subjects to interest and progression value
  • Avoid overloading the timetable unnecessarily
  • Confirm destination-specific expectations early
