Accelerated Option Pathway

GED pathway

The GED is an efficient high school equivalency credential for students seeking a faster route to university admission or career entry without following the traditional school timeline.

Alternative Pathway

Fact-01

GED covers four core subjects — Mathematical Reasoning, Reasoning Through Language Arts, Science, and Social Studies — and can be completed in as little as a few months.

Admission Guide

Admissions requirements

Efficient, test-based credential

The GED requires passing four computer-based subject tests. There are no coursework assignments or ongoing assessments — preparation is self-directed, and tests can be taken as soon as you are ready. Age eligibility and registration steps vary by country.

Requirements

  • Age eligibility (typically 16 or older)
  • Completed GED test registration
  • Government-issued photo identification
  • Testing center enrollment and scheduling
  • Preparation materials and practice test results

Student Guide

Prepare for the journey

01

Register and prepare

Create a GED account, schedule your subject tests, and use official practice materials to build readiness in all four subject areas.

02

Sit four subject tests

Complete the Mathematical Reasoning, Language Arts, Science, and Social Studies tests at an authorized testing center.

03

Apply with your credential

Use your GED credential to apply to colleges, universities, or career programs that accept high school equivalency qualifications.
